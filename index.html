LITERATURE REVIEW: Evaluate and report the literature related to your area of research to situate your work in the area and understand what’s already known in the area.

Primary sources: experimental papers, clinical trials
Secondary sources: review papers
Tertiary sources: textbooks, handbooks, dictionaries, encyclopedias

Use credible information sources. Refer to what others have researched and published before to support your ideas.
Cite your information sources and use a citation style consistently. Distinguish others’ works from your own by clearly stating your information sources.

LIBRARY RESOURCES - SUMMON / WEB OF SCIENCE / PUBMED

Summon: Search library collections (print and online) and more. Multidisciplinary. Journal articles, books, book chapters, magazine and newspaper articles. Curated by the library. Scholarly and credible information sources.

Web of Science: Multidisciplinary bibliographic database. Journal articles, review papers, proceedings papers, book chapters. Curated by subject experts. Keyword searching, but no controlled vocabulary.

PubMed Bibliographic database of biomedical literature. Journal articles, clinical trials, review articles. Includes MEDLINE (NLM database of citations and abstracts). Curated by subject experts. Can search with MeSH (Medical Subject Headings).
